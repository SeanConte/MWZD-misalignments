Modal Mereological Misalignments

This is an instantiation of a model formulated in Mayo-Wilson, Zollman, and Danks (2011), “The Independence Thesis: When Individual and Social Epistemology Diverge.”

Definitions

Model
K states / K actions: world q makes action q uniquely optimal.
Payoff variance: target variance among action success probabilities within each world.
Outcome: selected action yields binary payoff 0/1 with its world-relative success probability.
Strategy presets
Best-up-to-now: choose an action with maximal observed average payoff; ties are random.
Decreasing ε-greedy: explore randomly with probability 1/(t+1); otherwise choose best-up-to-now.
Social/fast ε-greedy: explore randomly with ε = max(floor, 1/(observed evidence+1)^α); otherwise choose best-up-to-now.
Preferred-action family: each agent has preferred action i mod K; with probability 1/(t+1), choose it; otherwise choose best-up-to-now.
Reinforcement learning: choose action a with probability w(a)/Σw; add observed payoffs to the corresponding action weights.
Metric definitions
Mean: average of all agent correctness cells at time t.
Coverage: fraction of histories where at least one agent chose the optimal action.
Jθ: fraction of histories where the proportion of correct agents is at least θ.
Alignment: average pairwise correlation of agents' correctness variables across histories.
SV: average within-history variance across agents.
MV: variance across histories of the column mean.
